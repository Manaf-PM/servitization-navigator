Many companies generate promising service ideas, but struggle to transform them into real product opportunities.

Typical challenges include:
- unclear value proposition
- weak alignment between marketing, product, and delivery
- difficulty selecting the right business model
- lack of structured evaluation and decision-making

Servitization Navigator addresses this gap by providing a simple, structured workflow to evaluate an idea, assess business-model readiness, and generate an actionable recommendation.

## Problem

In many B2B and industrial environments, ideas often emerge from marketing or business teams, but there is no clear framework to structure and evaluate them.

Teams struggle to assess strategic fit, understand internal readiness, and decide whether an idea should move forward. As a result, many initiatives remain vague, poorly prioritized, or disconnected from execution.

---

## Solution

Servitization Navigator is a web application designed to support the transition from idea to product opportunity.

It follows a simple and realistic workflow:

Idea → Assessment → Decision → Roadmap


### How it works

The product is built around a simple and realistic workflow that mirrors how product decisions are made in real organizations.

It starts with the opportunity itself. The user is asked to describe the idea in a structured way, including the value proposition, the target customer, the problem being solved, the expected revenue potential, and how the idea fits within the company’s broader strategy. This first step forces clarity and avoids working with vague or unqualified ideas.

Once the opportunity is defined, the system evaluates the company’s business model maturity through a structured assessment. This covers key dimensions such as the current business model structure, internal capabilities, and market context. The goal is to understand how prepared the organization is to support the proposed idea.

Based on this, a decision engine combines deterministic scoring with AI-generated interpretation. The scoring model provides a consistent classification of the company’s position and suggests the most relevant transition path, while the AI layer adds contextual insights and strategic recommendations tailored to the specific opportunity.

Finally, the product generates an actionable output. This includes an executive summary, a clear strategic rationale, a set of priority actions, an overview of key risks, and a 90-day roadmap designed to help move the idea toward validation and execution.

---

## Features

- Structured opportunity framing
- Business model maturity assessment (Product / Usage / Result)
- Deterministic scoring engine
- Opportunity scoring (0–10)
- GO / EXPLORE / NO GO decision support
- AI-generated recommendations
- 90-day execution roadmap
